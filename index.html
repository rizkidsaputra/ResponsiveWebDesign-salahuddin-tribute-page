Salahuddin Ayyubi

King Who Grants Victory

Here's a time line of Salahuddin's life:

  • 1137 - Born in Tikrit, Iraq, into a Kurdish family.
  • 1157 - Demonstrates military prowess and joins the army under the command of his uncle, Shirkuh, serving the Muslim rulers in the region.
  • 1169 - Establishes himself as a key military commander in Egypt during the period of political unrest.
  • 1171 - Takes control of Egypt, marking the foundation of the Ayyubid dynasty, as he strives to unify Muslim territories.
  • 1174 - Expands his influence by conquering Syria, consolidating his rule and creating a formidable Muslim state.
  • 1183 - Initiates campaigns to recapture strategic territories from the Crusaders, displaying strategic brilliance in military engagements.
  • 1187 - Achieves a pivotal victory at the Battle of Hattin, leading to the recapture of Jerusalem from the Crusaders, a significant moment in the history of the Crusades.
  • Late 1180s - Implements policies promoting religious tolerance and unity among Muslims in his territories.
  • 1191 - Engages in diplomatic negotiations with Richard the Lionheart during the Third Crusade, showcasing his diplomatic skills.
  • 1192 - Signs the Treaty of Jaffa with Richard the Lionheart, allowing Christians to visit Jerusalem peacefully, demonstrating a commitment to coexistence.
  • 1193 - Passes away in Damascus, mourned by both Muslim and Christian communities for his chivalry, integrity, and commitment to justice.

Conquer your enemy with your good conduct.

-- Salahuddin Ayyubi
